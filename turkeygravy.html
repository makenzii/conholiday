Turkey Gravy Recipe

What is Thanksgiving dinner without turkey or gravy? It seems turkey and gravy have made their way onto the table at the feast of the year and it's not going anywhere anytime soon. Check out our perfect Turkey Gravy recipe for the perfect condiment made from drippings of the main course. It's sure to transform your plate into a Thanksgiving dream feast!

  • Ingredients Needed
  • Drippings from 1 roasted turkey
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1-1 1/2 cups turkey or chicken stock
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Take the drippings from 1 roasted turkey and pour through a strainer into a glass container or two. This will separate out the meat bits from the liquid. Place the strained drippings in the fridge for 15 minutes so that the fats can solidify and rise to the top.
  2. Once the fat has solidified after 15 minutes, remove from the fridge. Remove the solidified fat and set aside for the next step. There should be just liquid drippings left in the jar now.
  3. Heat a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the reserved solidified fat, all-purpose flour and whisk together until smooth. Cook for 1 minute, whisking constantly, until bubbly and light-golden brown.
  4. Add in 1 cup of liquid turkey drippings. If you do not have a full cup, you may add the turkey or chicken stock to make up. Add in another cup of stock, for a total of 2 cups of liquid. Bring to boil, reduce heat and simmer and cook until the gravy has thickened to your preference. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve with your favorite Thanksgiving foods like mashed potatos, turkey, rolls, anything you want to put this delicious gravy on! Enjoy!

Tips: This recipe can be scaled up for larger crowds. Low sodium or unsalted stock options are recommended to keep manage sodium levels and saltiness. A teaspoon of cornstarch can help thicken your gravy if it is not thickening as you like. Likewise, extra stock can save an over-thickened gravy as well, just whisk in by the tablespoon until you get the consistency you like.
